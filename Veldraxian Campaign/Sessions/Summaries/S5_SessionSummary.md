# Session Five

Having just arrived in Stonebridge during the evening, the party sought to leave the Merchant's quarter and find a place to stay for the night. Traveling through the bustling city, many carts and people on the road as the group went towards the Business Quarter. They stopped at a relatively nice looking Inn, ~~Gang Place Street~~ Steven's Place where Shadow decided to keep looking for a cheaper Inn. Aeris paid no mind, heading inside and finding that the place was obviously a gang hide out and a place they stayed. He chose to ignore this, simply overpaying for a room and heading up. He would be rudely awakened in the middle of the night, as he heard several people break down the door of his neighbor as they were beaten over an owed debt. He did not sleep well after that, but hey the room was the cheapest he'd ever stayed at.

The rest of the party continued onward finding another Inn that seemed to be cheaper (but ended up being more expensive). The Yellow Wyvern Tavern it was called. Tolin and Shadow had an arguement at the entrance of the Tavern that led to the place's security to watch them due to a few of Tolin's comments (Quenlith silently sat far away from them), but they weren't kicked out. Tolin would of course torment everyone she came across before heading upstairs and sleeping on a bed instead of a table, startling the other two. In the morning her back was suspiciously not in pain.

They met up in the Gang Inn (Steven's Place) and met Aeris. While eating a bit of breakfast, the barkeep told the group that the Guildmaster may still be out of town, as he had recieved a King's Summon. The group would go to the Stonebridge Adventuring Guild and request to see the Guildmaster, delivering the Grandfather's letting meant for him.

About ten minutes later he all but rushed into the Guildhall, demanding to know where the party that delivered this letter was. He quickly rushed you into his office, something not typical of him based on the reactions of the staff. He let you known that he knew something of the mission they were on. He couldn't play favorites and directly aid you, but could help as he was able. He suggested the group reach out to another more expierenced party to aid them in training to continue growing stronger. The reccomended party was known as the **Bronze Dragon**, but they were currently out of town. In the meanwhile, just picking more jobs.

The party looked at jobs on the board, but chose to explore the city or visit certain shops afterwards and to meet back at the Guild a few hours later.
