# Session Eight
*The Best Solitaire Game Yet*

The session began back at the Stonebridge Adventurer's Guild, immediately returning to the impromptu party started by Tolin and Novadia. Tolin sang a catchy sea shanty in three different voices while Novadia played her instrument for the crowd, suddenly playing the instrument so fast she broke the strings on the thing (She's dubbed this music "Rush E"). Shadow and Quenlith wisely retreated outside to discuss more important matters.

Novadia would soon join them, where Shadow read aloud the letter from the Grandfather, which spoke of the warning signs that had been ignored the first time these events unfolded: animals behaving strangely, creatures and monsters appearing far from where they belonged, and attacks on villages becoming more frequent.

However, as interesting as that information was, Aeris' murder remained unsolved, and the party, now joined by Tolin, decided that would remain their priority.

Shadow led the group in search of a Brill Gang member known as **Drud**, operating on the northeastern side of Stonebridge.

As they approached, the party split into two groups. Shadow and Quenlith moved forward cautiously, observing Drud, and those watching over him. Meanwhile, Tolin and Novadia stayed behind and devised a distracted so absurd that nobody, including the DM, could have anticipated it.

The pair organized an impromptu illegal gambling ring centered around a series of games of solitaire. With young Novadia as the player and Tolin enthusiastically taking bets from a rapidly growing crowd.

Shadow would, after figuring out what was audibly going on down the street, would approach Drud with an offer not easily refused, saying "There's a lot of money to be made." He gestured to the increasingly loud crowd gathered around a young dragonborn playing cards while an elf loudly encouraged gambling on her chances of winning.

Drud, despite being handed the opportunity to walk away and simply enjoy some free money, instead, showing why he was labeled "not very bright" chose violence.

It was not an effective decision.

Drud and his associates were swiftling defeated. Two were killed, while drud and another were left unconscious. Shadow promptly involved the authorities, passing the now huge crowd on the way to a Guard Tower.

The arriving Guards were understandably confused, because before them stood:

 - Two unconscious gangsters, two dead
 - A large crowd gathered around an alleyway
 - An active betting ring
 - And several calm adventurers explaining the situation

Drud and his associate were taken back to the Guard Tower near the Military Quarter, where Shadow and the party were invited to participate in their interrogation.

The guards would also, unfortunately, have to dismantle the increasingly defeaning solitare gambling ring. Fortunately for Tolin and Novadia, they had already made a tidy profit.

---

Quick Summary:
The party investigated Aeris' murder, read the Grandfather's warning letter, tracked down Drud, distracted his operation with a gambling ring centered around Novadia playing solitaire, defeated Drud and his associates, and turned them over to the authorities.
