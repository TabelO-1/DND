## Letter
As you walk in you all notice a few adventurers in the guildhall, some looking at the job board, others simply around. There's a hound dog with one person, he looks agiated for some reason.

As Shadow turns in the job or they settle around the board. A letter is handed to the party, whomever comes to claim it. 

At Desk: to Shadow 
At board: a voice calls out "Sparrow Fledgings?" *A few heads turn to look at you all when the name is called.*

A Letter Bearing the Grandfather's Seal (A Wizard's Tower resmbling the Grandfather's Wizard Tower). As you open it and read it aloud:

*My Friends,*

*There is something I failed to give proper weight to when you were last here. At the time, I believed it a detail. In truth it was the beginning.*

*When we first faced these disturbances, they did not begin with great threats or clear dangers. There were no grand signs, no obvious enemy to name. Instead, it started small.*

*Animals behaved strangely. Creatures and monsters appeared in places they had no reason to be. Attacks on villages became more frequent, though these things never seemed connected.*

*We did not understand it then. Worse even, we did not treat it with the urgency it deserved. Only later, when we chose to follow those signs, did we begin to see where they led. Do not make the same mistake we did.*

*If you see these things, if something feels out of place, no matter how small - take notice. Follow it. These are not coincidences. They are the warning signs of something larger, though even now I cannot yet see its full shape.*

*I will continue my search from here. What I learn, I will send when I can.*

*Until then, grow stronger. But do not mistake strength for readiness alone. Awareness may serve you just as well.*

*A Final Note,*

*"Sparrow Fledgings." Perhaps an honest name, more fitting than you realize.*

- *G*

## Doggy Disturbance
A few moments after the letter is read -> dog acts up.

A trained hunting dog at the guild panics violently, snaps unpredicably, escaping its handler and attacking any who come close (Snapping at those who come close, lunging at those who approached, biting if threatened, trying to escape)

Goal: Calm -> Subdue (Initiative Time) if they fail to control or escalate

Animal Handling check: DC 16, lower than 4 and the dog will attack, initiative time.

To subdue: grapple the creature or just specify non-lethal damage.

If Calmed or Subdued, the Handler will thank the party, claiming it was strange for him to snap. He's usually incredibly tame and calm. If the dog is killed, the handler will be upset, but not at the party, simply glad it didn't get too far out of hand.

"He's usually very calm, though he started acting strangely this morning."
Follow up: "He wouldn't settle all of last night either. We were tracking in the woods before then."
