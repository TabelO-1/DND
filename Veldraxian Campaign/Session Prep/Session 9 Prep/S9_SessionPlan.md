Session 9 Plan — The Wolf Tavern

Opening: Guard Tower Interrogation

Start at the Guard Tower near the Military Quarter. Drud and the other captured Brill member are restrained. Eldid is present, irritated but willing to let the party ask questions because they delivered the suspects alive.

Drud is not brave. He is defensive, scared, and stupid enough to lie badly.

Useful answers from Drud:

He knows the Brill Gang keeps safehouses in the East Side. He knows one place called the Wolf Tavern. He insists it is “not the main place.” He says the boss is not there. If pressed about Aeris, he says he only heard there was “a bronze dragonborn problem” and that someone else handled it. He does not know who ordered it.

Give them this line if they need direction:

“Wolf Tavern. East Side. Old buildings. No one drinks there.”

If the party cooperates with Eldid, he offers two guards to accompany them at a distance. They will not storm the building unless things go loud. If the party excludes the guards, Eldid warns them that bodies in the East Side still become a guard problem. Either choice works.

Travel to the East Side

Describe the shift in the city. The streets narrow. Houses age. Fewer people linger outside. Curtains move, then still. The party should feel watched.

As they near the old buildings, give them the Wolf Tavern description:

Off the main road, several old buildings stand half-abandoned. One building is better maintained than the rest. Its faded sign reads Wolf Tavern, but there is no music, no laughter, no sound of mugs or conversation. Curtains are drawn. No patrons come or go.

It looks like a place that wants to be seen as open without actually being open.

Recon Options

Let them decide how to approach.

If they stake it out, after 10–20 minutes a Brill runner exits the back door. He has the “B” tattoo on his right hand. He is relaxed, alone, and not expecting trouble. If interrogated, he confirms it is a Brill place, says he is just a runner, and warns that if they are smart, they should not go through the front.

If they go through the front, the fake pub room has one member behind the bar and two sitting around. They immediately clock the party as strangers. One tries to slip into the back for reinforcements.

If they use the back door, there is one guard posted near the rear hall. He can be tricked, ambushed, or bypassed.

If they use an upstairs window, reward it. They enter near sleeping quarters and may avoid the front entirely.

Wolf Tavern Layout

The front room is a fake pub. There are unused tables, a bar, stale air, and no real tavern life. Three gang members are active here.

The back hallway leads to fake storage and offices. There are four potential reinforcements here, but don’t throw them all in at once. Add two if the front room raises an alarm, and two more only if the fight lasts more than five rounds or becomes extremely loud.

The office holds the lieutenant and one guard. The lieutenant wears a ring marked with a “B” and three stars. The guard’s ring has two stars.

The upstairs sleeping quarters contain three more members, but they should only matter if the party goes upstairs or the alarm drags on.

The basement is the real prize. It contains stolen goods, drugs, and the ledger.

Enemy Stats

Gang Member: AC 11, HP 6, dagger, +2 to hit, 1d4+1 damage.

Guard: AC 13, HP 12, shortsword, +2 to hit, 1d6+2 damage.

Lieutenant: AC 12, HP 20, longsword, +2 to hit, 1d8+2 damage, +2 to all ability checks/saves where relevant.

Run them like criminals, not soldiers. They stall, flee, call for backup, and protect evidence. They do not fight to the death unless trapped.

The Ledger

The lieutenant’s priority is the ledger. If combat turns against him, he tries to flee toward it or destroy it. He values keeping the ledger out of enemy hands more than his own safety.

The ledger contains names, payments, targets, and locations.

Important entries:

“Bronze Dragonborn — handled.”

East Row.

Filen Warehouse.

Steven’s Place.

Runner routes.

This confirms Aeris was deliberately targeted, and it gives the party the next lead without solving the whole gang.

Capturing the Lieutenant

If captured, he starts angry and defiant.

“You have no idea who you just stuck your nose into. You’ll pay for this.”

Minimal pressure gets Tier 1:

“Yeah, Brill runs this place.”

“It’s one of many.”

About Aeris: “Wrong place, wrong time. That’s all.”

Repeated pressure gets Tier 2:

“There are multiple safehouses.”

“They rotate locations.”

“You hit this place, another one fills its place tomorrow.”

Concerted effort or leverage with the ledger gets Tier 3. Give one major lead, not both unless the party earns it hard.

Location lead:

“Merchant’s Quarter. Filen Warehouse.”

Person lead:

“Marlo. He handles logistics.”

He will not give the full structure, major operations, or the leader’s location.

“You think I get told that?”

Guard Involvement

If guards are present, they hang back unless there is open violence. Afterward, Eldid can secure prisoners and evidence. This helps keep the party from becoming murder suspects.

If the party excludes the guards, they have more freedom but more risk. If they leave bodies, witnesses, or fire, the guards may arrive after and demand answers. This is not punishment; it is city consequence.

Session End Points

Best ending: the party finds the ledger and realizes Aeris’ death was part of something organized.

Second best ending: the lieutenant escapes with or without the ledger, leaving Filen Warehouse or Marlo as the next lead.

Emergency ending: if time runs short, end as they reach the Wolf Tavern and see the fake pub under the dim evening light.

Mental anchor for the session: this is not “bring down the Brill Gang.” This is “prove the Brill Gang is larger than one room.”
